1.  Project  Overview  and  Architectural  Framework  
The  Sikkim  High  Court  CMS  is  designed  as  a  workflow  engine  that  actively  moves  cases

1.1  Phased  Implementation  Timeline  
The  development  is  structured  across  three  distinct  phases  to  ensure  stability  and  institutional  
capacity

Phase  II  02.05.2026  –  30.09.2026  
Registry  &  AI  Introduction  
Basic  Registry  Dashboard;  Enhanced

Advanced  Registry  features  (Roster  Analysis);  Stabilization  of  AI  modules  for  courtroom  transcription.

 ●  Data  Consuming  Layer:  e-filed  data  is  pulled  from  the  web-based  CMS  into  the  CIS  1.0

"Data

●  Database  Mapping:  The  Django  backend  utilizes  “inspectdb”  utilities  to  map  existing

 ●  Filing  Number  Generation:  Once  an  e-filed  case  is  accepted  by  the  Scrutiny  Officer,  the

in

2.  Functional  Requirements:  Stakeholder  Dashboards  
The  system  provides  tailored  interfaces  for  the  specific  administrative  and  judicial  roles  within  
the
 
High
 
Court
 
establishment.
 
2.1  Dashboards  for  Hon'ble  Judges  and  Judicial  Staff  
Designed  to  facilitate  a  completely  paperless  courtroom,  the  judicial  interface  provides  real-time  
access
 
to
 
the
 
entire
 
case
 
bundle.
 
●  Multi-View  Case  Files:  Judges  can  toggle  between  Normal,  Flip,  and  Split  views  to  
maneuver
 
through
 
voluminous
 
records.
 
●  Digital  Tools:  Integrated  notepad  for  private  judicial  notes  and  the  ability  to  

highlight/annotate  documents  directly  within  the  electronic  file.  ●  Digital  Signatures:  Secure  signing  of  interim  orders  and  final  judgments,  which  are  then  
instantly
 
disseminated
 
to
 
the
 
advocate
 
dashboards.
 ●  Staff  Views:  Dedicated  views  for  PS/PA  to  Judges  to  manage  cause  lists  and  draft  orders  for  
judicial
 
approval.
 
2.2  Dashboard  for  Advocates  and  Litigants  
This  portal  serves  as  a  comprehensive  practice  management  tool  for  members  of  the  Sikkim  Bar  
Association.
 
●  Quick  e-Filing:  A  condensed  4-step  workflow:  Metadata  Entry  →  Document  Upload  
(searchable
 
PDF)
 
→
 
e-Payment
 
→
 
OTP/Aadhaar
 
Authentication.
 
●  Vakalath  Submission:  Digital  creation  and  acceptance  of  Vakalat  between  clients  and  
counsel.
 
●  Portfolio  Management:  A  searchable  database  of  all  cases  associated  with  a  specific  Bar  
ID,
 
including
 
real-time
 
status
 
updates
 
and
 
order
 
downloads.
 ●  My  Partners:  Ability  for  senior  advocates  to  add  juniors  or  clerks  as  partners  to  collaborate  
on
 
case
 
drafting.
 
2.3  Scrutiny  and  Registry  Dashboards  
These  internal  dashboards  manage  the  administrative  flow  of  cases  within  the  High  Court  
Registry.
 ●  Automated  Scrutiny  (Phase  II):  Machine-learning  modules  to  flag  common  technical

(formatting,

●  Real-time  Communication:  Integrated  chat  feature  allowing  Filing  Scrutiny  Officers  
(FSO)

●  Roster  &  Cause  List  (Phase  III):  Automated  listing  of  cases  based  on  the  judicial  roster

●  Registry  Sections:  Specific  modules  for  the  Writ,  Criminal,  and  Civil  sections  to  track  the

3.  Technical  Architecture  and  Security  3.1  Tech  Stack  
●  Frontend:  Angular  (SPA)  for  responsive  dashboard  interfaces.
 
●  Backend:  Django  (Python)  for  robust  workflow  logic  and  security  management.  ●  Database:  PostgreSQL  (RDBMS)  utilizing  schemas  to  isolate  judicial  data  from  
administrative
 
logs.
 

 
3.2  Security  and  Compliance  
As  a  government  establishment,  the  CMS  must  strictly  adhere  to  CERT-In  directions.
 
●  Log  Management:  All  system  and  application  logs  must  be  maintained  securely  within  
Indian
 
jurisdiction
 
for
 
a
 
rolling
 
period
 
of
 
180
 
days
.
 
●  Incident  Reporting:  Any  unauthorized  access  or  cybersecurity  incident  must  be  reported  to  
CERT-In
 
within
 
6
 
hours
 
of
 
discovery.
 
●  Authentication:  Mandatory  multi-factor  authentication  (MFA)  for  all  judicial  and  registry  
accounts.
 
●  Audit  Trails:  Tamper-evident  audit  trails  for  every  document  action,  ensuring  
non-repudiation.
 
4.  Data  Migration  and  Transition  
Phase  I  focuses  on  migrating  data  from  the  existing  High  Court  e-Filing  3.0  portal  to  the  new  
indigenous
 
system.
 
●  Migration  Scope:  Successfully  registered  cases  with  assigned  CNR  numbers  will  be  
migrated
 
with
 
their

metadata
 
and
 
document
 
history.
 
●  Data  Cleaning:  Deduplication  of  litigant  and  advocate  identities  to  ensure  a  "Single  Person  
View"
 
across
 
the
 
High
 
Court
 
database.
 
●  Downtime  Protocols:  During  migration,  e-filing  may  be  temporarily  suspended,  with  
provisions
 
for
 
physical
 
filing
 
to
 
ensure
 
access
 
to
 
justice
 
is
